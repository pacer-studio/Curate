Mirakl stays. SAP goes. Shopify Plus bridges the gap.

A technical overview of the two-platform architecture for Reem Mall / Curate: system ownership, integration approach, and Shopify Plus capabilities.

Client: Reem Mall / Curate Prepared by: Pacer Studio Date: March 2026 Focus: Shopify Plus and Mirakl Integration Architecture

The migration from SAP Commerce to Shopify Plus is a storefront swap, not a platform overhaul. Mirakl is not being replaced, reconfigured, or handed to a new implementation partner. It continues to run every marketplace operation it runs today: seller onboarding, commission calculation, payout logic, order routing, promotions settlement, and returns processing.

What changes is the customer-facing layer. SAP Commerce is replaced by Shopify Plus as the storefront, checkout, and product experience platform. The two systems are joined by the Mirakl Connect operator connector (a Shopify app built and maintained by Mirakl) which keeps products, inventory, and orders in sync between them.

Architecture Overview

Two platforms, one marketplace

The architecture separates cleanly into two layers: the marketplace engine and the customer experience layer, connected by a certified integration. Each platform does what it is built for.

Mirakl: Marketplace Engine

Mirakl is the system of record for all marketplace commercial operations. It owns the business logic that makes the marketplace function: how commissions are calculated, how promotions are funded, how sellers are onboarded, how payouts are scheduled, and how returns are financially settled. None of this is touched by the migration.

Seller onboarding · Commission calculation · Order routing · Payout and settlement · Promotions funding · Returns processing · Vendor SLA enforcement · Operator invoicing

Shopify Plus: Customer Experience Layer

Shopify Plus replaces SAP Commerce as the storefront, checkout, and customer experience platform. It handles everything the customer sees and interacts with, including VAT calculation at checkout, multi-currency display, and brand-aligned checkout flows. It receives product data from Mirakl and sends confirmed orders back to Mirakl for routing and fulfilment.

Storefront and navigation · Product display pages · Checkout and payment · VAT calculation · Multi-currency · SEO and CMS · Buyer-facing invoicing · UX and brand experience

Mirakl Connect: The Bridge

Mirakl Connect is the official operator connector built by Mirakl for Shopify. It is not a custom integration or third-party middleware. It is a certified Shopify app maintained by Mirakl itself. The connector handles two flows: products and inventory sync from Mirakl into Shopify listings (so Shopify always reflects the current Mirakl catalogue), and orders flow from Shopify back into Mirakl on payment confirmation (so Mirakl can route, calculate commission, and manage fulfilment). The connector replaces the SAP Commerce to Mirakl integration without requiring any changes to Mirakl's internal configuration.

Shopify Plus Capabilities

What Shopify Plus delivers at the Plus tier

Shopify Plus goes well beyond a storefront. At the Plus tier, the platform provides native tools for multi-currency selling, UAE VAT compliance, custom checkout logic, and operational automation, all relevant to Curate's operating model.

Shopify Markets: multi-currency and regional pricing

A single storefront can serve multiple currencies and regions with localised pricing, language, and tax rules. For a UAE marketplace with international vendors, this handles currency display and collection natively, with no custom development required.

Shopify Tax: UAE VAT compliance

Shopify Tax calculates VAT at checkout automatically, tracks VAT liability across all orders, and generates reports for filing. Buyer-facing VAT-compliant invoices are handled via Shopify-native invoice apps (Sufio, Order Printer Pro). Marketplace-to-seller invoicing remains Mirakl's domain.

Shopify Functions: custom checkout logic

Shopify Functions allow custom business logic to run server-side inside the checkout: discount validation, delivery method filtering by vendor, and custom order routing rules. This is not bolt-on JavaScript; it is a Shopify-native extension framework covering checkout logic that sits outside Mirakl's scope.

Shopify Flow: no-code operations automation

Flow is a no-code workflow automation tool available exclusively on Plus. It triggers actions on order, customer, or product events: for example, tagging orders by vendor, sending operational alerts, or calling downstream APIs. It reduces manual operational overhead without custom development.

Multi-location fulfilment

Shopify Plus natively supports multiple fulfilment locations. For Curate's consignment model (stock held centrally, shipped from warehouse), Shopify manages the physical inventory record while Mirakl determines which seller fulfils. The two systems co-operate cleanly; neither overrides the other.

Checkout Extensibility

Checkout Extensibility on Plus allows branded, compliant checkout UI customisation without forking Shopify's checkout codebase. It supports custom fields, post-purchase pages, and brand-aligned experiences, areas that required significant custom development effort on SAP Commerce.

API-first architecture

Shopify Plus exposes the full Storefront API, Admin API, and Webhooks API: the same interfaces that Mirakl Connect uses to sync products and receive orders. No custom middleware needs to be built. The certified connector uses these APIs directly, making the integration reliable and maintainable.

B2B on Shopify

Native B2B features including company accounts, net payment terms, volume pricing, and draft orders, are included on Shopify Plus with no additional licensing. If Curate has a trade or B2B customer segment, this is available out of the box.

Vendor Shopify connector

For vendors who run their own Shopify stores, Mirakl provides a third-party connector (developer.mirakl.com/content/connector/third-party/shopify) that syncs their catalogue and inventory directly into Mirakl. This removes the need for manual data entry into the seller portal and simplifies onboarding for Shopify-native vendors.

Common Questions

Questions about this approach

A few questions come up naturally when reviewing a two-platform architecture. These are addressed directly below.

By Design Does this require a Mirakl implementation partner?

A Mirakl systems integrator is needed when implementing Mirakl from scratch: configuring commission structures, building payout schedules, and setting up seller SLA workflows. Curate already has Mirakl implemented and operating. The work required here is not reimplementing Mirakl. It is connecting Shopify to Mirakl using the certified operator connector. Pacer handles this connection. If Mirakl's configuration needs to evolve independently (new commission tiers, new seller onboarding workflows), that is a parallel Mirakl workstream and not a dependency for the Shopify migration.

Mirakl-Owned How are vendor commissions, payouts, and promotions affected?

They are not affected. Mirakl owns all of these functions end-to-end and continues to do so after the migration. Commission calculation (variable rates by category, volume, and fulfilment model), promotional payout funding (operator vs. seller split), and vendor settlement schedules all live in Mirakl's operator configuration. When orders arrive via the connector from Shopify instead of SAP Commerce, Mirakl processes them identically. None of this logic is re-implemented, migrated, or altered as part of this project.

Needs Planning How does the product catalogue move across?

The correct migration path runs through Mirakl, not directly from SAP to Shopify. Products that already exist in Mirakl's catalogue are synced automatically into Shopify by the connector, with no manual product upload required. Products currently held only in SAP need to be onboarded into Mirakl first, after which the Shopify sync is automatic. This makes catalogue migration primarily a Mirakl data ingestion exercise. We will produce a dedicated migration plan scoping the SAP to Mirakl data audit and ingestion work.

Shopify Plus What about VAT, invoicing, and total cost of ownership?

UAE VAT at checkout is handled by Shopify Tax natively. Buyer-facing VAT invoices are generated via a Shopify-native invoice app included in the project scope. Marketplace-to-seller invoicing remains Mirakl's domain and does not change. On total cost of ownership: Shopify Plus is priced at $2,300/month (~AED 100–110K per year), a fixed and known cost. Mirakl licensing costs are unchanged by the storefront migration; this is an existing cost Curate carries regardless. A full Year-1 TCO breakdown covering licences, apps, and development will be included in the revised proposal.

Recommended Approach

The path forward

The migration is a well-defined storefront swap with a clear two-phase delivery. Mirakl is not reconfigured. The connector replaces the SAP to Mirakl integration point.

Core principle: We do not touch Mirakl. We connect to it. Mirakl's existing configuration (commissions, payouts, promotions, returns, SLAs) remains the operator's domain throughout. The Mirakl Connect operator connector, built and maintained by Mirakl, handles the bridge.
